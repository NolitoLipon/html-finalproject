About Magellan's Cross

Magellan's Cross is a historic landmark located in Cebu City, Philippines, marking the spot where Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan planted a cross in 1521 to symbolize the arrival of Christianity in the Philippines. The cross is housed in a small chapel, built in 1740, which is located just beside the Basilica Minore del Santo Niño. It is believed that the original cross is encased in a wooden structure to protect it from deterioration, and the one visible to the public is a replica. Magellan's Cross is an important symbol of Cebu’s rich history, particularly in the spread of Catholicism in the country. The site attracts thousands of visitors and pilgrims each year, making it a significant cultural and religious destination. Surrounding the cross, visitors can also explore the vibrant local markets and other historical landmarks, further enriching the experience. Magellan's Cross stands as a reminder of the deep historical ties between the Philippines and European exploration, as well as the enduring influence of Christianity in the nation.
